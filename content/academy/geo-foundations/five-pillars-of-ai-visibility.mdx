# The Five Pillars of AI Visibility

AI visibility isn't one thing — it's five. Each pillar represents a distinct signal that AI models use to decide whether to recommend a brand. Neglect any one, and you leave a gap your competitors will fill.

## Pillar 1: Citations (25% of AI Visibility Score)

**What it is:** Being mentioned in forum threads — Reddit, Quora, Facebook Groups — that AI models reference when generating answers.

**Why it matters:** When someone asks Perplexity "What's the best CRM for small agencies?" and a top Reddit thread recommends your brand, that recommendation flows directly into the AI's answer. It's the most direct path from action to AI visibility.

**What good looks like:** Your brand is mentioned authentically in 15+ high-authority threads across your key topics, with those threads ranking on Google page 1.

**What bad looks like:** Your competitors are mentioned in 50+ threads and you're in zero. AI models literally have no source material to cite you from.

## Pillar 2: AI Presence (30% of AI Visibility Score)

**What it is:** The direct measurement of whether AI models mention your brand when asked buying-intent questions in your category.

**Why it matters:** This is the north star metric. Everything else — citations, entities, reviews, press — exists to move this number. Share of Model (SOM) tells you whether your GEO efforts are actually working.

**What good looks like:** 20%+ SOM across key models, with your brand recommended (not just mentioned) in multiple contexts. Positive sentiment in mentions.

**What bad looks like:** 0-5% SOM. AI models either don't know you exist or only mention you when specifically asked about you by name.

<Callout type="tip">
AI Presence is weighted most heavily (30%) because it's the outcome metric. The other four pillars are the inputs that drive it.
</Callout>

## Pillar 3: Entity Consistency (15% of AI Visibility Score)

**What it is:** Whether your brand's information — name, description, category, contact details — is consistent across every platform AI models check.

**Why it matters:** AI models cross-reference sources. If your LinkedIn says you're a "marketing agency," your Google Business Profile says "digital advertising firm," and Crunchbase says "technology company," the AI model loses confidence. Inconsistency creates doubt, and doubt means the model recommends your competitor instead.

**What good looks like:** One canonical description deployed across all platforms. Schema markup on your website. Consistent NAP (name, address, phone) everywhere. A Google Knowledge Panel.

**What bad looks like:** Different descriptions on every platform, missing profiles on key directories, no schema markup, outdated information on Crunchbase or LinkedIn.

## Pillar 4: Reviews (15% of AI Visibility Score)

**What it is:** Your presence and reputation on review platforms — Google Reviews, Trustpilot, G2, Capterra, and industry-specific sites.

**Why it matters:** AI models use reviews as trust signals. A brand with 500 positive reviews across 5 platforms looks far more trustworthy than one with 3 reviews on a single platform. Review volume, recency, and sentiment all factor into AI recommendations.

**What good looks like:** 100+ reviews across 3+ platforms, 4.0+ average rating, reviews added in the last 30 days, and a healthy response rate to negative reviews.

**What bad looks like:** Fewer than 10 total reviews, no presence on major review platforms, or old reviews with no recent activity.

## Pillar 5: Press & Earned Media (15% of AI Visibility Score)

**What it is:** Third-party media coverage — news articles, guest posts, podcast appearances, industry lists, and press releases that mention your brand.

**Why it matters:** Press coverage is one of the strongest authority signals. When multiple publications write about your brand independently, AI models interpret this as "this brand is noteworthy and trusted." It's the difference between self-promotion and independent validation.

**What good looks like:** Regular mentions in industry publications, thought leadership content from your team, coverage in major outlets, and backlinks from high-authority domains.

**What bad looks like:** Zero press mentions in the last 12 months, no third-party validation, and all brand mentions coming from your own website.

## How the AI Visibility Score Works

The composite AI Visibility Score is a weighted average of all five pillars:

| Pillar | Weight | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| Citations | 25% | Highly actionable, direct path to AI mentions |
| AI Presence | 30% | The outcome metric — did it actually work? |
| Entities | 15% | Foundational, less volatile |
| Reviews | 15% | Trust signals AI models rely on |
| Press | 15% | Authority signals from independent sources |

A score of 0-30 means you're largely invisible. 30-60 means there are significant gaps but some foundation. 60-80 means you're competitive. 80+ means you're dominating your category.

<Callout type="info">
The score isn't just a vanity metric — it's a diagnostic tool. A low citation score with a high review score tells you exactly where to focus: citation seeding first, reviews can wait.
</Callout>
