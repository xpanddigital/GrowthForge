# What Is GEO?

If your brand doesn't show up when someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity for a recommendation in your space, you're invisible to a rapidly growing segment of buyers. Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) fixes that.

## The Shift: Search to AI Answers

For 25 years, search worked the same way. A user typed a query, Google returned a list of links, and the user clicked through. SEO was about getting your link as high as possible on that list.

That model is breaking down. Today, millions of people ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google's AI Overviews for direct answers. These AI models don't return a list of links — they synthesize an answer and cite their sources. If your brand isn't one of those sources, you're not in the conversation.

**GEO is the practice of making your brand appear in AI-generated answers.**

## What AI Models Need to Recommend You

AI models aren't random. They follow patterns when deciding which brands to mention. There are three core signals:

### 1. Authority
The model needs evidence that your brand is legitimate and trusted. This comes from press coverage, high-authority websites mentioning you, and consistent brand information across directories.

### 2. Consensus
Multiple independent sources need to say good things about you. If Reddit threads, Quora answers, review sites, and news articles all mention your brand positively, the AI model has "consensus" that you're worth recommending.

### 3. Recency
AI models weight recent information more heavily. A Reddit thread from last week mentioning your brand carries more weight than a blog post from 2021. Fresh citations, recent reviews, and current press coverage all signal that your brand is actively relevant.

<Callout type="info">
Think of it like this: Authority is your resume, consensus is your references, and recency is proof you're still in the game. You need all three.
</Callout>

## How GEO Differs from SEO in One Sentence

SEO gets your website ranked in Google's link list. GEO gets your brand named in AI models' answers. They use different signals, target different surfaces, and require different tactics — but they amplify each other.
