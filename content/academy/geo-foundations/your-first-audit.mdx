# Running Your First AI Visibility Audit

The audit is where everything starts. It scans all five pillars of AI visibility, scores your brand out of 100, and generates a prioritized action plan. Whether you're onboarding a new client or pitching a prospect, the audit is your diagnostic tool.

## What the Audit Does

The AI Visibility Audit runs five scans in parallel:

1. **Citation Scan** — Searches Google for your keywords, finds Reddit/Quora/Facebook threads that rank, and checks whether your brand is mentioned in any of them
2. **AI Presence Test** — Sends buying-intent prompts to ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Claude, and checks whether your brand appears in their answers
3. **Entity Audit** — Searches for your brand across key platforms (Google Business, LinkedIn, Crunchbase, etc.) and checks for consistency
4. **Review Scan** — Checks your presence and ratings on Google Reviews, Trustpilot, G2, Capterra, and industry-specific sites
5. **Press Scan** — Searches Google News and the web for third-party mentions of your brand in the last 12 months

The whole process takes 3-5 minutes.

## Step-by-Step Walkthrough

### Step 1: Select Your Client

Navigate to Audits and click **Run New Audit**. Select the client you want to audit. Make sure the client has:
- A completed brand brief (this tells the AI what to look for)
- At least 5-10 active keywords (these drive the discovery scans)
- Competitor names listed (optional, but makes the report much more valuable)

<Callout type="warning">
If keywords aren't set up yet, the citation and AI presence scans will have nothing to search for. Add keywords first — the AI Monitor keyword setup guide covers this in detail.
</Callout>

### Step 2: Choose Audit Type

- **Full Audit** — All 5 pillars. Best for onboarding and quarterly reviews. Takes 3-5 minutes.
- **Quick Audit** — Citations + AI Presence + Entities only. Skips reviews and press. Takes 1-2 minutes.
- **AI Presence Only** — Just tests AI models. Useful for quick pulse checks between full audits.

For your first audit, always run a **Full Audit**. You need the complete picture.

### Step 3: Watch It Run

You'll see a real-time progress screen as each pillar completes. Each pillar shows its score as soon as it finishes — you don't have to wait for all five.

### Step 4: Read Your Results

The results dashboard shows:

**The Big Number** — Your composite AI Visibility Score (0-100). This is the weighted average of all five pillar scores. Most brands score 15-35 on their first audit.

**Pillar Breakdown** — Five score bars showing each pillar individually. This tells you where your strengths and gaps are. A low citation score (say 12/100) with a moderate review score (say 55/100) means citation seeding should be your first priority.

**Executive Summary** — An AI-generated narrative that explains your scores in plain language, highlights the biggest findings, and compares your brand to competitors. This is what you'd share with a client or prospect.

**Action Plan** — A prioritized list of specific actions, ranked by impact-to-effort ratio. Each action tells you:
- What to do
- Which pillar it addresses
- Expected impact (high/medium/low)
- Effort required
- Which MentionLayer module handles it

<Callout type="tip">
The action plan is designed so you can work top to bottom. Priority #1 is always the highest-impact, lowest-effort action. Start there and work down the list.
</Callout>

## Understanding Common Score Patterns

**Low everything (15-25 composite):** This is typical for brands that haven't done any GEO work. The good news — there's massive upside. Start with citation seeding (fastest wins) and entity cleanup (lowest effort).

**High reviews, low everything else (30-40 composite):** The brand has built review presence organically but hasn't invested in AI-specific signals. Citation seeding and press campaigns will have outsized impact because the trust foundation is already there.

**Moderate citation score, low AI presence (35-50 composite):** The brand is mentioned in some threads but AI models aren't picking it up yet. This usually means the citations aren't in the right threads (not high-authority enough) or entity signals are muddying the waters.

## Using the Audit as a Sales Tool

The audit is your most powerful sales weapon. Here's the playbook:

1. Run a free audit for a prospect before the sales call
2. Walk them through the results: "You scored 22/100 for AI visibility. Your top competitor scored 58."
3. Show the action plan: "Here are the 6 specific things we'd do in the first 90 days"
4. Close with the metric: "We track Share of Model monthly. You'll see your AI visibility score climb from 22 to 50+ within 90 days."

The audit makes the invisible problem visible. Prospects can't unsee a gap once you've shown it to them.

## Re-running Audits

Run a full audit:
- At client onboarding (baseline)
- Monthly during active campaigns (progress tracking)
- Quarterly for maintenance clients (health check)

Each audit is saved as a snapshot. The dashboard shows your composite score trending over time — this is how you prove ROI.
