# How AI Models Find Answers

Not all AI models work the same way. Understanding how each one decides what to recommend is the foundation of any GEO strategy. Get this wrong, and you'll optimize for the wrong signals.

## The Big Five AI Models

### ChatGPT (OpenAI)
ChatGPT has two modes. Its base knowledge comes from training data — a massive snapshot of the internet up to its cutoff date. But it also uses Bing search to pull real-time results when it needs current information.

**What it cites:** Reddit threads, Wikipedia, news articles, high-authority blogs, and Bing search results. It favors sources with strong domain authority and recent engagement.

### Perplexity
Perplexity is the most important model for GEO because it's built entirely around live web search. Every answer includes explicit source citations with clickable links.

**What it cites:** Real-time web results, Reddit, Quora, news sites, forums, and documentation. It weights recency heavily — fresh content wins.

### Google Gemini & AI Overviews
Google's AI uses the same index that powers traditional search. If you rank in Google, you're more likely to appear in Gemini's answers and AI Overviews.

**What it cites:** Google's own index — websites, Knowledge Panels, Google Business Profiles, YouTube, and Reddit (Google has a content licensing deal with Reddit).

### Claude (Anthropic)
Claude's knowledge comes primarily from training data. It doesn't do real-time web search by default, though it can access tools and search when configured.

**What it cites:** Training data sources — Wikipedia, documentation, well-known publications, and high-authority content that was prominent at training time.

<Callout type="tip">
Perplexity and Google AI Overviews are the highest-priority targets for most businesses because they use real-time data. Changes you make today can show up in their answers within days.
</Callout>

## Why Reddit and Quora Dominate AI Citations

Here's a pattern that surprises most people: AI models cite Reddit and Quora at disproportionately high rates. There are three reasons:

1. **Google ranks them highly.** Reddit threads consistently appear on page 1 for buying-intent queries. Since Perplexity and Gemini pull from Google's index, those threads flow directly into AI answers.

2. **They contain authentic opinions.** AI models are trained to value authentic human experiences over marketing content. A Reddit user saying "I tried X and it solved my problem" carries more weight than a brand's own landing page.

3. **They're structured for extraction.** Threads with questions and upvoted answers are perfectly structured for AI models to extract recommendations from.

## Training Data Lag vs Real-Time Retrieval

This distinction matters for your strategy:

**Training data lag** means some models (like ChatGPT's base knowledge and Claude) only know about content that existed when they were trained. If your brand got major press coverage yesterday, these models won't know about it until their next training update — which could be months.

**Real-time retrieval** means models like Perplexity and Google AI Overviews can find and cite content that was published today. This is where citation seeding has the fastest impact.

### The Practical Takeaway

Your GEO strategy needs to work on two timescales:
- **Short-term (days to weeks):** Citation seeding in Reddit/Quora threads and press coverage will appear in Perplexity and Google AI Overviews quickly
- **Long-term (months):** Building authority, review volume, and entity consistency improves your brand's presence in training data for the next model updates across all AI platforms

## What "Share of Model" Means

Share of Model (SOM) is the metric that ties this all together. It measures: **out of all relevant buying-intent prompts tested, what percentage of AI responses mention your brand?**

For example, if you test 40 prompts across 4 AI models (10 prompts each), and your brand appears in 8 of those responses, your SOM is 20%.

SOM benchmarks vary by industry:
- **0-5%:** Invisible — AI models don't know you exist
- **5-15%:** Emerging — you show up occasionally
- **15-30%:** Competitive — you're in the conversation
- **30%+:** Dominant — you're the go-to recommendation

<Callout type="info">
Most brands start with 0-5% SOM. The goal of a GEO campaign is to systematically push that number up over 90 days, using all five pillars.
</Callout>
