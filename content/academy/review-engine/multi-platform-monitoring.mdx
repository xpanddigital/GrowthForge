# Multi-Platform Review Monitoring

Reviews are scattered across a dozen platforms. Checking each one manually is tedious and slow — by the time you see a negative review on Trustpilot, it's been sitting there for two weeks. MentionLayer aggregates everything into one feed so nothing slips through.

## The Review Feed

The review feed is a unified, chronological stream of every review across all monitored platforms. Each review shows:

- **Platform** — Where the review was posted (Google, G2, Trustpilot, etc.)
- **Rating** — Star count (normalized to 5-star scale)
- **Sentiment** — Positive, neutral, or negative (AI-analyzed from review text)
- **Date** — When the review was posted
- **Text preview** — First few lines of the review content
- **Response status** — Whether you've responded, and if so, when

You can filter by platform, sentiment, rating, date range, or response status. The default view shows unresponded reviews first — these are your action items.

<Callout type="tip">
Check the review feed daily for the first week to establish a baseline, then shift to 2-3 times per week. Set up alerts for negative reviews so you can respond within 24 hours — response speed matters for both the customer and the AI signal.
</Callout>

## Sentiment Tracking Over Time

Beyond individual reviews, the Review Engine tracks sentiment trends:

- **Monthly average rating** — Is your overall rating improving or declining?
- **Sentiment distribution** — What percentage of reviews are positive, neutral, negative?
- **Topic analysis** — What are people praising or complaining about most?
- **Velocity chart** — Are you getting more or fewer reviews per month?

These trends matter for AI visibility. A brand whose sentiment is improving over time looks better to AI models than one with a static or declining trend.

## Alert Setup

Configure alerts to notify you when:

- **Any negative review** is posted (1-2 star) — respond within 24 hours
- **Review velocity drops** below your target — may need to re-activate review campaigns
- **New platform detected** — your brand appears on a platform you weren't monitoring
- **Competitor milestone** — a competitor passes a review count threshold

Alerts can go to email, Slack, or in-app notifications. For agencies managing multiple clients, email alerts with client name and platform are the most efficient.

## Why Response Speed Matters

Responding to reviews quickly serves two purposes:

### Customer Experience
A fast, professional response to a negative review can turn a detractor into a promoter. It shows you care and are actively engaged.

### AI Signal
AI models can see your review responses. A brand that consistently responds to reviews — especially negative ones — appears more engaged and trustworthy. Some AI models specifically factor in response rate and quality when building brand entity assessments.
