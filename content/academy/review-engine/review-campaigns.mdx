# Review Generation Campaigns

You can't just wait for reviews to show up. Proactive review generation — asking happy customers to share their experience — is how brands build the review volume that AI models need to recommend them confidently.

## Setting Up a Review Campaign

A review campaign systematically reaches out to customers and asks them to leave a review on a specific platform. The key elements:

### Recipient Selection
Choose customers who are most likely to leave a positive review:
- Recent purchasers (within the last 30 days)
- Customers who've had positive support interactions
- Long-term customers with high usage
- Customers who've already expressed satisfaction informally

### Platform Selection
Direct reviewers to the platform that matters most for your AI visibility. If your G2 profile has 12 reviews and Google has 150, send the next batch to G2.

### Timing
When you send the request matters:
- **Post-purchase:** 3-7 days after delivery or service completion
- **Post-milestone:** After the customer achieves a result with your product
- **Post-support:** After a successful support ticket resolution
- **Renewal:** When a customer renews or upgrades

<Callout type="tip">
The best time to ask for a review is immediately after a positive moment — a successful onboarding, a support ticket resolved, a feature they love. Strike while the experience is fresh.
</Callout>

## The Campaign Workflow

### Step 1: Select Recipients
Choose from your customer list or import a CSV of email addresses. MentionLayer helps you filter by recency, engagement, and likelihood of positive response.

### Step 2: Choose Template
Select a review request template or customize one. The template should:
- Be brief (3-4 sentences max)
- Explain why reviews matter (social proof, helping others make decisions)
- Include a direct link to the review platform
- Be personal — use their name, reference their specific experience if possible

### Step 3: Set Timing
Choose when to send — immediately, or schedule for a specific time. For B2B, weekday mornings work best. For consumer brands, evenings and weekends often have higher conversion rates.

### Step 4: Send
MentionLayer sends the review requests via email. Each recipient gets a unique tracking link so you can measure who opened, clicked, and left a review.

### Step 5: Track
Monitor the campaign dashboard for:
- Send rate (emails delivered)
- Open rate (typically 30-50%)
- Click-through rate (typically 10-20%)
- Review conversion rate (typically 5-15%)

## Compliance: The Rules

Review generation must follow platform guidelines and ethical standards:

### Never Incentivize
Don't offer discounts, gifts, or rewards in exchange for reviews. Most review platforms explicitly prohibit this, and it undermines the authenticity AI models value.

### Always Genuine
Only ask customers who've actually used your product. Never send review requests to people who haven't had a real experience with your brand.

### Don't Filter by Sentiment
You can time your requests strategically (asking after positive moments), but you should never screen out people who might leave negative feedback. Authentic review profiles include some criticism.

### Respect Opt-Outs
If someone says they don't want to be asked for reviews, remove them from future campaigns immediately.

### Platform-Specific Rules
- **Google** prohibits review gating (asking how they feel first, then only directing positive responders to leave a review)
- **G2** allows incentives for "honest" reviews but has specific guidelines
- **Trustpilot** has strict invitation-only collection rules
- Check each platform's current guidelines before launching campaigns

<Callout type="warning">
Review gating — asking customers to rate their experience first and only sending satisfied customers to the review platform — violates most platform guidelines. Send everyone to the review platform regardless of their likely sentiment.
</Callout>

## Campaign Cadence

For sustainable review growth:

- **Ongoing drip:** 5-10 requests per week, targeting recent customers
- **Monthly burst:** 20-30 requests after a product launch or major update
- **Post-event:** Batch requests after webinars, conferences, or seasonal peaks

Aim for a steady trickle of reviews rather than big spikes. Platforms may flag accounts that go from 0 reviews to 50 in a week.
