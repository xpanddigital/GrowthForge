# Competitor Review Intelligence

Your competitors' reviews are a goldmine of strategic intelligence. They reveal what customers love and hate about competing products — information you can use in both your GEO campaigns and your product positioning.

## Tracking Competitor Reviews

MentionLayer monitors competitor review profiles alongside yours. For each focus competitor, you can track:

- **Total review count** across platforms
- **Average rating** and rating trend
- **Review velocity** — how many new reviews per month
- **Sentiment distribution** — positive, neutral, negative ratios
- **Platform presence** — which review sites they're active on

This data updates with each monitoring cycle, giving you a real-time picture of how competitors' review presence compares to yours.

## Finding Sentiment Gaps

The most actionable intelligence comes from analyzing what competitors' reviewers complain about. These complaints represent opportunities:

### In Citation Responses
When you're writing citation responses for forum threads, competitor weaknesses make natural talking points: "I tried [Competitor] but the [specific issue from their reviews] was a dealbreaker. Switched to [Your Brand] and the [specific strength] is exactly what I needed."

This isn't making things up — it's referencing real customer feedback that's publicly available.

### In Press Pitches
Competitor review trends can inform press angles: "While [Competitor] reviews frequently cite [issue], our data shows 94% customer satisfaction on exactly that dimension." Data-driven differentiation is catnip for journalists.

### In Product Messaging
If competitors consistently get dinged for poor customer support, that's your signal to lead with "white-glove support" in your messaging. Let their weakness define your strength.

<Callout type="tip">
Sort competitor reviews by most recent and lowest rating to find their current pain points. Issues from last month are more relevant than issues from two years ago — they reflect the current product experience and are more likely to be in AI training data.
</Callout>

## The Review Gap Analysis

MentionLayer calculates the review gap between you and each competitor:

| Metric | You | Competitor A | Gap |
|--------|-----|-------------|-----|
| Total reviews | 45 | 1,200 | -1,155 |
| Average rating | 4.6 | 4.1 | +0.5 |
| Platforms present | 2 | 5 | -3 |
| Monthly velocity | 3 | 15 | -12 |

This tells you exactly where to focus. In this example, you can't close the volume gap quickly, but you can:
- Expand to more platforms (close the platform gap)
- Increase your velocity (close the velocity gap)
- Maintain your rating advantage (your quality edge)

## Using Competitor Data in Monthly Reports

When reporting to clients, competitor review data adds context:

"Your average rating (4.6) is higher than any competitor, but your review volume (45) is well below the category average (380). This month's review campaign generated 12 new reviews, closing the gap by 3%. Recommended action: accelerate review campaigns to reach 100 reviews by Q3."

Clients understand competitive comparisons. Showing them where they stand — and what you're doing to improve it — makes the value of your GEO service tangible.

## Competitive Review Alerts

Set alerts for competitor review milestones:

- Competitor passes a review count threshold (100, 500, 1000)
- Competitor's average rating drops below a threshold (useful for press angles)
- Competitor gets a surge of negative reviews (opportunity to differentiate)
- Competitor appears on a new review platform

These alerts help you react to competitive shifts rather than discovering them in a monthly review.

## Ethical Boundaries

Use competitor review data for legitimate competitive intelligence only:

- **Do** reference publicly available competitor weaknesses in your citation responses
- **Do** use competitor review trends to inform your press angles
- **Do** track competitive review metrics for reporting
- **Don't** write fake negative reviews about competitors
- **Don't** impersonate competitor customers
- **Don't** report genuine competitor reviews as fake to get them removed
