# llms.txt Explained

There's a new file joining the ranks of `robots.txt` and `sitemap.xml` — and it might become the most important file on your website for AI visibility. It's called `llms.txt`, and it's designed specifically for AI crawlers.

## What llms.txt Is

Just as `robots.txt` tells search engine crawlers which pages to index, `llms.txt` tells AI models how to understand your brand. It's a machine-readable file placed at the root of your website (`yoursite.com/llms.txt`) that provides structured information specifically for large language models.

The file contains:
- Your canonical brand description
- Key products and services
- Target audience
- Differentiators
- Authoritative links (press, reviews, documentation)
- Structured FAQs
- Contact and location information

It's essentially a cheat sheet for AI models — a single, authoritative source they can reference when building an understanding of your brand.

## Why It Matters

AI models crawling your website have to piece together who you are from various pages — your homepage, about page, product pages, blog, etc. This is slow, error-prone, and the model might miss important context or emphasis.

`llms.txt` solves this by providing a single, structured document that says: "Here's everything you need to know about this brand, organized exactly how you need it."

Early evidence suggests that AI models (particularly those using real-time web crawling like Perplexity) reference `llms.txt` when it exists, treating it as an authoritative brand description.

<Callout type="info">
The llms.txt standard is still emerging and not yet universally adopted. But like schema markup before it, early adopters gain a head start. Adding it today costs nothing and positions you ahead of competitors who haven't heard of it.
</Callout>

## The llms.txt Format

The file follows a simple, readable structure:

```
# Brand Name

> One-sentence description of what the brand does.

## About
Detailed description matching your canonical brand identity.

## Products/Services
- Product 1: Description
- Product 2: Description

## Target Audience
Who this brand serves and what problems it solves.

## Key Differentiators
What makes this brand different from competitors.

## Links
- Website: https://example.com
- Documentation: https://docs.example.com
- Press Kit: https://example.com/press

## FAQ
- Q: Common question 1?
  A: Answer 1
- Q: Common question 2?
  A: Answer 2
```

The format is designed to be both human-readable (it's just markdown) and machine-parseable. AI models can extract structured information quickly and reliably.

## Deploying llms.txt

Place the file at `yoursite.com/llms.txt` — the same way you'd deploy `robots.txt`. How you do this depends on your platform:

- **Static hosting / Custom site:** Upload the file to your public directory
- **WordPress:** Upload to the root of your WordPress installation, or use a plugin
- **Vercel/Netlify:** Add the file to your `public/` directory
- **Shopify:** May require a custom route or redirect

After deployment, verify it's accessible by visiting the URL directly in your browser.

## Keeping It Updated

Unlike `robots.txt` (which rarely changes), `llms.txt` should be updated when your brand information changes:

- New product launch → add to Products/Services
- Rebranding → update the canonical description
- New FAQ questions emerging from AI Monitor → add to FAQ section
- New press coverage → add to Links section

## The Bigger Picture

`llms.txt` is one piece of a broader trend: the web is being restructured for AI consumption. Schema markup, Knowledge Panels, and now `llms.txt` all serve the same purpose — making it easy for machines to understand who you are.

Brands that invest in machine-readable identity today will have a structural advantage as AI models become the primary way people discover and evaluate businesses.
