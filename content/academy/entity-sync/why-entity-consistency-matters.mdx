# Why Entity Consistency Matters for AI

When AI models answer a question about your industry, they don't just pull from one source. They cross-reference dozens of platforms to build a "picture" of your brand. If those platforms disagree about who you are, the AI loses confidence — and recommends your competitor instead.

## How AI Models Build Brand Entities

Think of how AI models form an understanding of a brand:

1. They read your website
2. They check your Google Business Profile
3. They look at your LinkedIn company page
4. They reference directories like Crunchbase, G2, or Capterra
5. They pull from your schema markup and structured data
6. They factor in Wikipedia/Wikidata if you have entries

From all of these sources, the model constructs a mental model — an "entity" — of what your brand is, what it does, and how trustworthy it is.

**When all sources agree, the entity is strong.** The AI model is confident about who you are and is willing to recommend you.

**When sources disagree, the entity is weak.** The AI model doesn't know which description to trust, so it defaults to brands with stronger, more consistent entities.

## The Consistency Problem

Most businesses don't realize their brand information is inconsistent. Here are the most common issues:

### Different Descriptions
Your LinkedIn says "We're a digital marketing agency." Your Google Business Profile says "SEO and web design company." Your Crunchbase profile says "marketing technology startup." These are close enough that a human wouldn't notice — but AI models see three different businesses.

### Outdated Information
You rebranded 18 months ago, but your old name and description still appear on Yelp, industry directories, and that Crunchbase profile nobody updated.

### Missing Platforms
You have a website and a Google Business Profile, but no LinkedIn company page, no Crunchbase entry, and no presence on G2 or Capterra. AI models have fewer data points to work with, making your entity weaker.

### Conflicting Details
Your website says you were founded in 2018. Crunchbase says 2019. Your LinkedIn says 2017. Minor? To a human, yes. To an AI model building an entity, it's noise that reduces confidence.

<Callout type="warning">
Entity inconsistency is a silent killer. Your SOM might be low not because of weak citations or poor press coverage, but because AI models can't confidently identify who you are. Always check your entity score before investing heavily in other pillars.
</Callout>

## The Knowledge Panel Connection

Google's Knowledge Panel — that info box that appears when someone searches your brand name — is the purest expression of entity strength. If Google can build a Knowledge Panel for you, it means your entity signals are strong enough for a structured understanding.

Brands with Knowledge Panels tend to have higher AI visibility because the same signals that create the panel (consistent information across platforms) also feed AI model confidence.

No Knowledge Panel? That's a signal your entity needs work.

## What Entity Consistency Does for SOM

Fixing entity consistency doesn't create dramatic overnight SOM jumps like citation seeding can. Instead, it raises the floor:

- It ensures that when AI models do encounter your brand, they can confidently identify and recommend it
- It removes friction — inconsistency actively works against your other GEO efforts
- It compounds with other pillars — citations + strong entity = higher recommendation rate

Think of it as tuning the engine. Citation seeding is the fuel, press is the turbo, but entity consistency is making sure the engine runs clean.
