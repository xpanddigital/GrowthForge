# Directory Audit Walkthrough

Your brand exists on more platforms than you think — and many of those listings are outdated, incomplete, or inconsistent. The directory audit finds every platform where your brand appears and scores how well they match your canonical identity.

## Which Directories Matter for AI

Not all directories carry equal weight with AI models. The platforms that matter most are the ones AI models actively reference:

### Tier 1 (High Impact)
- **Google Business Profile** — Referenced by Gemini and Google AI Overviews directly
- **LinkedIn Company Page** — Frequently cited in professional/B2B contexts
- **Wikipedia/Wikidata** — The gold standard for entity recognition
- **Crunchbase** — Referenced for company details, founding info, funding

### Tier 2 (Moderate Impact)
- **G2, Capterra, TrustRadius** — Referenced for software/SaaS comparisons
- **Trustpilot** — Referenced for consumer trust signals
- **Yelp** — Referenced for local businesses
- **Industry-specific directories** — Varies by vertical

### Tier 3 (Supporting)
- **Twitter/X, Facebook, Instagram** — Social profiles that confirm brand identity
- **Better Business Bureau** — Trust signal for US businesses
- **Glassdoor** — Referenced for employer brand (affects B2B perception)

## How the Audit Works

MentionLayer's directory audit runs in three steps:

### Step 1: Discovery
Searches Google for your brand name and collects the top 20+ results. Identifies which platforms your brand appears on and which key directories are missing.

### Step 2: Extraction
For each discovered platform, extracts the brand description, category, contact information, and any other structured details.

### Step 3: Scoring
Compares each platform's information against your canonical brand identity. Scores each platform on:
- **Description match** — How closely does the description align with your canonical?
- **Information completeness** — Are all fields filled in?
- **Accuracy** — Is the information current and correct?

The overall consistency score is the weighted average across all platforms.

<Callout type="tip">
Don't be alarmed by a low initial consistency score. Most businesses score 40-60% on their first audit because platforms accumulate outdated information over time. The important thing is knowing where the gaps are so you can fix them.
</Callout>

## Reading the Consistency Report

The report shows each platform with a status:

- **Consistent** (green) — Description and details match your canonical. No action needed.
- **Partial match** (amber) — Some information matches, some is outdated or different. Update needed.
- **Inconsistent** (red) — Significant differences from your canonical. Priority fix.
- **Missing** (gray) — No presence on this platform. Consider creating a listing.

For each platform, the report shows exactly what differs: "LinkedIn description says 'digital marketing agency' but canonical says 'AI visibility platform.'"

## The Fix Task List

MentionLayer generates a prioritized task list from the audit:

Each task includes:
- **Platform** to update
- **What to change** (specific field and new value)
- **Impact rating** (high/medium/low based on platform tier)
- **Effort estimate** (typically 5-15 minutes per platform)

Tasks are sorted by impact, so you start with the highest-value fixes first. A Google Business Profile update is always higher priority than fixing a secondary directory listing.

## Executing the Fixes

For most platforms, fixing a listing means:

1. Log into the platform with your admin credentials
2. Navigate to profile/business settings
3. Update the description, category, and details to match your canonical
4. Save and wait for the platform to approve changes (some have review periods)

Some updates are instant (LinkedIn), some take days for review (Google Business Profile), and some require claiming the listing first (Crunchbase, Yelp).

<Callout type="warning">
Don't copy-paste the exact same text to every platform. Use the platform-adapted versions from your canonical. Each platform has different length limits and emphasis areas. The goal is consistent facts, not identical text.
</Callout>

## Creating Missing Listings

For platforms where you have no presence, the task list will suggest creating a listing. Prioritize:

1. Google Business Profile (if applicable)
2. LinkedIn Company Page
3. G2/Capterra (for SaaS/software)
4. Crunchbase (for funded companies)
5. Industry-specific directories

Each new listing strengthens your entity by giving AI models another consistent data point.

## Re-auditing

After fixing your listings, re-run the directory audit to verify. It takes 2-3 minutes and shows your updated consistency score. Aim for 80%+ consistency before moving on to other GEO priorities.

Schedule quarterly re-audits to catch drift — platforms can change categories, descriptions can be overwritten, and new directories may emerge.
