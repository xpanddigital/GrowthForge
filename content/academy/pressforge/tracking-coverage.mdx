# Tracking Press Coverage

Getting press coverage is only half the job. Tracking it — knowing where you were mentioned, by whom, and what impact it had on AI visibility — is how you prove ROI and refine your strategy.

## How MentionLayer Monitors for Coverage

PressForge continuously scans for new mentions of your brand across:

- **Google News** — Catches articles in news publications
- **Google Web Search** — Catches guest posts, blog features, and non-news articles
- **Specific publications** — If you pitched 30 journalists, MentionLayer watches those publications specifically

Monitoring runs automatically after pitches are sent and continues for 30 days per campaign.

## Coverage Classification

When coverage is detected, MentionLayer classifies it:

| Type | What It Is | AI Impact |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| Feature article | Dedicated piece about your brand/story | Highest |
| Mention in roundup | Listed among several brands in a category article | High |
| Expert quote | Your spokesperson quoted in a broader article | High |
| Brief mention | Brand name appears in passing | Moderate |
| Press release pickup | Your release republished verbatim | Low |

Feature articles and expert quotes carry the most weight because they represent editorial decisions — a journalist chose to write about you or quote you. Press release pickups carry less weight because they're automated republication.

## Measuring Press Impact on AI Visibility

This is where PressForge connects to AI Monitor. For each piece of coverage, MentionLayer tracks:

### Direct Citation Tracking
Does any AI model cite the article where you were covered? If Perplexity references a TechCrunch article that mentions your brand, that's a direct press-to-AI-visibility pipeline.

### SOM Correlation
Does your SOM change after coverage is published? The timeline view overlays press coverage dates with SOM trends so you can see the correlation.

### Source Attribution
When AI Monitor detects new brand mentions, it checks whether the cited sources include any of your press coverage. This creates a direct attribution chain: "This AI mention came from this article, which came from this campaign."

<Callout type="tip">
Press coverage on Perplexity-visible sources (news sites, major publications) creates the fastest AI visibility impact. Coverage on sites that aren't indexed by Perplexity or Google takes longer to influence AI responses.
</Callout>

## Unlinked Mention Detection

Not all press coverage includes a link to your website. MentionLayer tracks both:

- **Linked mentions** — The article includes a hyperlink to your site
- **Unlinked mentions** — Your brand name appears but without a link

For SEO, unlinked mentions have limited value. For GEO, they're nearly as valuable as linked ones because AI models extract brand information from text, not just links.

That said, unlinked mentions represent a link-building opportunity. Reach out to the journalist or publication and ask if they'd be willing to add a link — many will, since you're asking for something simple that improves their article.

## Building Coverage Reports

For client reporting, PressForge generates coverage summaries that include:

- **Total mentions** in the reporting period
- **Publication list** with authority tiers
- **Coverage types** breakdown (features, mentions, quotes)
- **Linked vs unlinked** ratio
- **AI visibility impact** — SOM change correlated with coverage
- **Competitor coverage comparison** — how your press volume compares

These reports can be exported as part of your monthly client reporting or as standalone press performance summaries.

## Press Coverage as Ongoing Asset

Press coverage compounds over time:

- Articles stay indexed in Google for years
- AI models reference archived articles alongside recent ones
- Each piece of coverage makes the next one easier (journalists see you've been covered elsewhere)
- Your brand entity grows stronger with each independent mention

This is why press velocity matters — each month of consistent coverage adds to a growing asset that continues to influence AI visibility long after publication.
