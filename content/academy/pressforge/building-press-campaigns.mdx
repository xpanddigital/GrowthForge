# Building Press Campaigns

A press campaign isn't just "write a press release and hope." It's a structured effort to generate earned media coverage around a specific angle, targeted at specific publications and journalists.

## Campaign Types

MentionLayer supports four campaign types, each suited to different situations:

### Product Launch
**When:** You're releasing a new product, feature, or major update
**Angle:** What it does, who it helps, what problem it solves
**Target publications:** Industry press, tech press, product review sites
**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks from announcement

### Thought Leadership
**When:** Your team has unique expertise or data worth sharing
**Angle:** Expert perspective on an industry trend, original research, contrarian take
**Target publications:** Industry publications, business press, podcasts
**Timeline:** Ongoing — aim for 1-2 placements per month

### Data Study
**When:** You have original data or insights from your platform/customers
**Angle:** "We analyzed X and found Y" — journalists love original data
**Target publications:** General business press, industry press, data-focused outlets
**Timeline:** 3-6 weeks including data preparation

### Expert Commentary
**When:** There's breaking news or trending topics in your industry
**Angle:** Your team provides expert analysis on current events
**Target publications:** Anyone covering the story — speed matters
**Timeline:** 24-72 hours — this is reactive, not planned

<Callout type="tip">
Data studies generate the most backlinks and citations per effort. If you have any proprietary data — usage statistics, market trends, survey results — package it as a study. Journalists cite original data heavily, and AI models pick up those citations.
</Callout>

The idea generation considers:

## Setting Up a Campaign

### Step 1: Choose Campaign Type
Select the type that matches your current opportunity. Most brands should start with thought leadership (easiest to execute) and add data studies as they build momentum.

### Step 2: Define the Angle
Write a one-sentence pitch: "We're helping agencies measure AI visibility with the first Share of Model tracking tool." This becomes the hook for every pitch.

### Step 3: Draft the Press Release
MentionLayer generates a draft press release from your angle, brand brief, and campaign type. Edit it to add specific details, quotes, and data points. The AI gives you structure — you add the substance.

### Step 4: Build Your Journalist List
Select journalists from MentionLayer's discovery tool or add your own contacts. Target 20-40 journalists per campaign — enough for coverage probability, not so many that you're spamming.

### Step 5: Generate Personalized Pitches
MentionLayer generates a personalized email pitch for each journalist based on their recent coverage and beat. These aren't templates — each pitch references why this story matters to that specific journalist's audience.

### Step 6: Send, Follow Up, Track
Send pitches, follow up after 48-72 hours if no response, and track who opens, replies, and publishes.

## Campaign Workflow

The full lifecycle:

```
Idea → Release Draft → Journalist Selection → Pitch Generation →
Send → Follow Up → Coverage → Track Impact on SOM
```

Each stage is tracked in PressForge with status updates. You can see at a glance which campaigns are active, which pitches are pending, and which have resulted in coverage.

## Campaign Cadence

For maximum AI visibility impact, aim for:

- **1 active campaign per month** for single brands
- **1 campaign every 2 weeks** for agencies managing multiple clients
- **Reactive commentary** whenever relevant news breaks (bonus, not scheduled)

Consistency is more important than volume. One well-executed campaign per month builds steady press velocity — the signal AI models weight.
