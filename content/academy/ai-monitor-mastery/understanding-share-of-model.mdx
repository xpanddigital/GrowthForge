# Understanding Share of Model

Every GEO campaign needs a north star metric. For AI visibility, that metric is Share of Model — and if you're not tracking it, you're flying blind.

## What Share of Model Actually Measures

Share of Model (SOM) answers one question: **when people ask AI models buying-intent questions in your category, how often does your brand appear in the answer?**

The formula is simple:

**SOM = (prompts where your brand is mentioned / total prompts tested) x 100**

If you test 50 prompts across multiple AI models and your brand shows up in 8 of those responses, your SOM is 16%.

## Why SOM Is the North Star

SOM is the outcome metric — everything else in GEO exists to move this number. Citation seeding, entity cleanup, press campaigns, review generation — they're all inputs. SOM is the output that tells you whether those inputs are actually working.

Think of it like this:
- **SEO's north star** = organic traffic
- **PPC's north star** = ROAS
- **GEO's north star** = Share of Model

## What "Good" Looks Like

SOM benchmarks vary by industry and competitive density, but these ranges hold for most B2B and B2C categories:

| SOM Range | Status | What It Means |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| 0-5% | Invisible | AI models don't know you exist |
| 5-15% | Emerging | You show up occasionally, usually only on one model |
| 15-30% | Competitive | You're in the conversation across multiple models |
| 30-50% | Strong | You're a go-to recommendation |
| 50%+ | Dominant | You own your category in AI search |

Most brands score 0-5% on their first measurement. That's normal — and it means there's massive upside.

<Callout type="info">
Don't panic about a low initial SOM. The value isn't the number itself — it's having a baseline to measure improvement against. A brand that goes from 3% to 18% in 90 days has a clear ROI story to tell.
</Callout>

## SOM vs Mentioned vs Recommended

MentionLayer tracks three levels of brand presence in AI responses:

1. **Mentioned** — Your brand name appears anywhere in the response. This includes passing references like "companies like X, Y, and Z."
2. **Recommended** — The AI model specifically suggests or endorses your brand. This is stronger than a mention — the model is saying "you should check out X."
3. **Linked** — The AI model includes a URL pointing to your website or a page about you. This is the strongest signal and most common in Perplexity responses.

SOM counts mentions (the broadest measure). But the recommendation rate and link rate are tracked separately because they tell you about the quality of your visibility, not just the quantity.

## Why You Track Over Time, Not as a Snapshot

A single SOM measurement is a starting point. The real value comes from trending:

- **Weekly tracking** shows whether your GEO actions are moving the needle
- **Monthly comparisons** smooth out noise and show real trajectory
- **Before/after specific actions** proves which tactics work (e.g., "SOM jumped from 8% to 14% two weeks after we seeded 20 Reddit threads")

AI model responses aren't static — they change as models update their training data and as live-search results shift. A brand can gain or lose SOM without doing anything, purely because competitors acted.

## The Competitor Dimension

SOM isn't just about your brand — it's relative. Knowing your competitor's SOM tells you:

- **Who's winning** in each AI model
- **Which models** are the biggest opportunity (if competitors dominate ChatGPT but nobody owns Perplexity, that's your opening)
- **Whether the gap is growing or shrinking** over time
