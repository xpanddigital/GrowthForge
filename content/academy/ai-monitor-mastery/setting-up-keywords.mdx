# Setting Up Keywords for Monitoring

The keywords you choose for AI monitoring determine what you measure. Get them right, and your SOM score reflects reality. Get them wrong, and you're tracking noise.

## What Makes a Good Monitoring Keyword

Not all keywords belong in your AI Monitor. The best monitoring keywords share three traits:

### 1. Buying Intent
You want keywords that represent someone actively looking for a solution — the kind of query where an AI recommendation leads to a purchase or signup.

**Good:** "best CRM for small agencies," "music licensing services for indie artists"
**Bad:** "what is a CRM," "history of music licensing"

The first set reflects someone ready to buy. The second is informational — even if AI mentions your brand, it's unlikely to convert.

### 2. Category-Level Terms
Monitor keywords should represent your category, not just your brand name. You already know AI models mention you when someone asks about you by name. The question is: do they mention you when someone asks about the *category*?

**Good:** "best project management tools," "affordable web hosting"
**Bad:** "Asana features," "GoDaddy pricing"

### 3. Competitor Comparison Queries
These are gold for monitoring because they're exactly the context where AI models decide between brands:

**Good:** "Notion vs Asana," "alternatives to Mailchimp," "HubSpot competitors"

<Callout type="tip">
Aim for 10-20 keywords per client. Fewer than 10 and your SOM sample is too small to be meaningful. More than 20 and you're diluting focus and spending unnecessary credits.
</Callout>

## How MentionLayer Generates Prompt Variations

Here's something most people miss: AI models respond differently to different phrasings of the same question. Ask ChatGPT "best email marketing tools" and you might get a different brand list than "what email marketing platform should I use?"

MentionLayer solves this by generating **5 prompt variations per keyword**, each with a different phrasing style:

1. **Direct question:** "What are the best {keyword}?"
2. **Recommendation request:** "Can you recommend a good {keyword}?"
3. **Comparison format:** "Compare the top {keyword} options"
4. **Problem-solution:** "I need help with {keyword} — what are my options?"
5. **Alternative seeking:** "What are some alternatives for {keyword}?"

This means 10 keywords generate 50 unique prompts, tested across multiple AI models. That's a statistically meaningful sample for your SOM calculation.

## Adding Keywords

To add keywords to AI Monitor:

1. Navigate to **AI Monitor > Keywords**
2. Click **Add Keyword**
3. Enter the keyword phrase
4. MentionLayer auto-generates 5 prompt variations — review and edit if needed
5. Choose which AI models to test against (default: all)
6. Save

You can also bulk-import keywords from a CSV or pull from your existing keyword list on the client's profile.

## Recommended Keyword Categories

For most businesses, build your keyword list across these categories:

| Category | Examples | Why |
|----------|---------|-----|
| Core category terms | "best {category} tools" | The broadest measure of visibility |
| Use-case specific | "best {tool} for {use case}" | Tests visibility in specific contexts |
| Competitor alternatives | "alternatives to {competitor}" | Where brand switching happens |
| Problem-oriented | "how to solve {problem}" | Buying-intent from pain points |
| Industry-specific | "{category} for {industry}" | Tests niche visibility |

## When to Add or Remove Keywords

**Add keywords when:**
- You launch a new product or service line
- You discover a new competitor appearing in AI results
- Client feedback reveals new buying-intent phrases their customers use

**Remove keywords when:**
- A keyword consistently returns irrelevant results (AI models interpret it differently than intended)
- You've stopped offering a service that keyword relates to
- The keyword is too broad to produce actionable data
