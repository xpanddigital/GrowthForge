# Competitor Tracking

You don't operate in a vacuum. Knowing your competitors' AI visibility — and how it changes over time — is what turns raw SOM data into competitive strategy.

## How MentionLayer Discovers Competitors

When AI Monitor runs your prompts, it doesn't just look for your brand. It extracts every brand mentioned in every AI response. Over time, this builds a comprehensive picture of who shows up in your category.

These auto-discovered competitors are ranked by how frequently they appear. The brands that show up most often in your keyword space are your true AI competitors — and they might be different from your traditional SEO or market competitors.

<Callout type="info">
Don't be surprised if your AI competitors differ from your market competitors. A small company with aggressive Reddit presence and good press coverage can outrank a market leader in AI recommendations. AI visibility rewards signals, not size.
</Callout>

## Setting Focus Competitors

From the auto-discovered list, select 3-5 **focus competitors** for detailed tracking. MentionLayer will:

- Track their SOM alongside yours on every monitoring run
- Show head-to-head comparison on each keyword
- Alert you when a competitor's SOM changes significantly
- Identify keywords where they dominate and you don't

Choose focus competitors based on who appears most often in AI responses for your keywords — not who you compete with most in traditional sales.

## Reading the Competitor Comparison

The competitor comparison chart shows SOM side-by-side:

**Your brand vs competitors, per model:** This reveals which AI models favor which brands. If a competitor dominates ChatGPT but you're stronger on Perplexity, that's useful for prioritization.

**Your brand vs competitors, per keyword:** This is where you find specific gaps. If Competitor A owns "best CRM for agencies" but nobody owns "CRM for creative agencies," that keyword is your opening.

**SOM trend over time:** Watch for competitors whose SOM is rising quickly. They're likely running their own GEO campaigns, and you need to respond before they lock in their position.

## Turning Competitor Data into Action

Competitor tracking isn't just observation — it feeds directly into your campaign strategy:

### 1. Find Uncontested Keywords
Sort keywords by competitor SOM. Keywords where no competitor exceeds 15% are low-competition opportunities. Citation seeding here will have outsized impact because there's no entrenched leader.

### 2. Identify Their Citation Sources
When a competitor has high SOM for a keyword, look at the AI responses to see which sources are being cited. Are Reddit threads mentioning them? Press articles? Review sites? Those same sources are your targets — get your brand into those threads and publications.

### 3. Exploit Their Weaknesses
If a competitor has high SOM but negative sentiment, that's an opportunity. AI models may recommend them by default today, but positive citations about your brand can shift that positioning.

### 4. Monitor Their Velocity
A competitor whose SOM jumped 10 points in a month is actively running GEO campaigns. Watch where their growth is coming from and defend your strong keywords before they encroach.

## Competitor Alerts

Set up alerts for significant competitor movements:

- **SOM spike:** A competitor's SOM increases by more than 5 points in a single monitoring period
- **New entrant:** A brand you haven't seen before starts appearing in 10%+ of responses
- **Keyword takeover:** A competitor overtakes you on a keyword where you were previously leading

These alerts help you react quickly rather than discovering competitive shifts weeks later in a monthly review.
