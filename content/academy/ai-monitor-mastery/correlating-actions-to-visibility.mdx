# Correlating Actions to Visibility Changes

The whole point of GEO is cause and effect: you take actions, and AI models respond by mentioning your brand more. But the connection isn't instant — understanding the lag times and attribution patterns is critical for proving ROI.

## The Correlation Timeline

Different GEO actions have different lead times before they show up in SOM:

| Action | Perplexity Impact | ChatGPT Impact | Google AI Overviews |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|---------------------|
| Citation seeding (Reddit/Quora) | 1-2 weeks | 1-3 months | 2-4 weeks |
| Press coverage published | 1-3 days | 1-3 months | 1-2 weeks |
| Entity consistency fixes | 2-4 weeks | 1-3 months | 2-4 weeks |
| Review volume increase | 2-4 weeks | 1-3 months | 2-4 weeks |
| Schema markup added | 2-4 weeks | N/A | 1-2 weeks |

**Perplexity** is your fastest feedback loop because it uses live web search. A Reddit thread you post in today can appear in Perplexity's answers within days.

**ChatGPT** is the slowest because it relies on training data updates. Actions you take today might not show up for months — but when they do, they tend to persist.

**Google AI Overviews** falls in between, using Google's live index which updates regularly but not instantly.

<Callout type="tip">
Use Perplexity SOM as your leading indicator and ChatGPT SOM as your lagging indicator. If Perplexity SOM is climbing but ChatGPT hasn't moved yet, that's expected — ChatGPT will catch up at its next training update.
</Callout>

## What Causes SOM to Go Up

These are the proven drivers of SOM improvement:

### New Citations in High-Authority Threads
The single biggest driver. When your brand gets mentioned in a Reddit thread that ranks on Google page 1 for a buying-intent keyword, AI models pick it up — especially Perplexity and Google AI Overviews which use live web data.

### Press Coverage in Authoritative Publications
A feature article or mention in an industry publication creates a strong authority signal. Multiple publications mentioning your brand builds the "consensus" signal AI models need.

### Review Volume and Recency
Platforms like Trustpilot and G2 are frequently cited by AI models. Increasing your review count — especially recent reviews — strengthens the trust signal.

### Entity Consistency Improvements
Fixing inconsistent brand descriptions across platforms helps AI models build a coherent "entity" for your brand. This reduces confusion and increases confidence in recommendations.

## What Causes SOM to Go Down

SOM can drop even without you doing anything wrong:

- **Competitors acting:** If a competitor runs an aggressive citation campaign, their share goes up and yours goes down relatively
- **Content going stale:** Old Reddit threads lose visibility as newer content takes their place in Google rankings
- **Training data updates:** When ChatGPT updates training data, the sources it relies on can shift
- **Negative press or reviews:** Bad coverage can flip sentiment from positive to negative, causing AI models to recommend competitors instead

## Building the Attribution Story

When reporting to clients or justifying GEO spend, you need to connect specific actions to SOM changes. Here's the framework:

### 1. Timestamp Your Actions
Log when you post citations, publish press releases, fix entity listings, or launch review campaigns. MentionLayer timestamps all of this automatically.

### 2. Overlay Actions on the SOM Timeline
The AI Monitor timeline view shows SOM trend with action markers. You can visually see: "We seeded 15 threads in week 3, and SOM on Perplexity jumped from 8% to 16% in week 5."

### 3. Control for Variables
Be honest about attribution. If SOM jumped but you also got organic press coverage the same week, both contributed. The timeline helps you separate signal from noise.

### 4. Use A/B Keyword Groups
For sophisticated attribution, monitor some keywords where you're actively running campaigns alongside "control" keywords where you're not acting. If campaign keywords see SOM lift but control keywords don't, that's strong evidence your actions caused the change.

## The Weekly GEO Rhythm

The most effective GEO campaigns follow a consistent weekly cycle:

**Monday:** Review SOM from the latest monitoring run. Note any significant changes up or down.

**Tuesday-Wednesday:** Analyze the data. Which keywords moved? Which competitors gained? What actions from previous weeks might have caused the shift?

**Thursday:** Plan this week's actions. Prioritize based on the biggest SOM gaps and highest-impact opportunities.

**Friday:** Execute. Seed citations, launch press pitches, fix entity listings, send review requests.

**Repeat.** Consistency beats intensity. Seeding 5 threads per week for 12 weeks outperforms seeding 60 threads in one burst.

<Callout type="info">
Don't measure SOM daily — the sample size per day is too small. Weekly monitoring gives you a meaningful signal. Monthly reporting smooths the noise for client presentations.
</Callout>
