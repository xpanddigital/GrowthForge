# Reading Your AI Monitor Results

Data is useless unless you know how to read it. The AI Monitor dashboard packs a lot of information into one screen — here's how to extract the insights that matter.

## The Visibility Score Card

The big number at the top of your dashboard is your **AI Visibility Score** — a weighted composite that factors in SOM, recommendation rate, model coverage, and sentiment. It's scored 0-100.

This isn't the same as raw SOM. The visibility score includes quality signals:
- A brand mentioned in 20% of prompts but never recommended scores lower than one mentioned in 15% but recommended in half of those
- A brand with positive sentiment scores higher than one with neutral mentions
- Coverage across multiple models scores higher than dominance in just one

Think of the visibility score as your "GPA" and SOM as one of the grades that feeds into it.

## Model Breakdown

Below the score, you'll see how each AI model treats your brand:

| What to look for | What it means |
|-------------------|--------------|
| High SOM on Perplexity, low on ChatGPT | Your live web presence is strong but training data hasn't caught up |
| High on ChatGPT, low on Perplexity | You have historical authority but recent citations are weak |
| Zero across all models | No AI-accessible signals exist — start with citation seeding |
| Strong on Google AI Overviews only | Your SEO is carrying you, but purpose-built GEO signals are missing |

Each model pulls from different data sources, so uneven performance is normal. Use the breakdown to prioritize where to focus.

<Callout type="tip">
Perplexity is your leading indicator — it uses real-time web data, so changes from citation seeding show up here first. ChatGPT is your lagging indicator — it needs training data updates to reflect new citations.
</Callout>

## Keyword Performance Table

This is where the actionable insights live. The keyword table shows your SOM for each monitored keyword:

**High SOM keywords** (20%+) — These are your strengths. Protect them by maintaining citation freshness and monitoring for competitor moves.

**Medium SOM keywords** (5-20%) — These are your opportunities. You're in the conversation but not dominant. Targeted citation seeding on these topics will have the highest ROI.

**Zero SOM keywords** (0%) — These are your gaps. AI models have no source material connecting your brand to these topics. You need to create that connection through citations, press, or both.

Sort by SOM ascending to find your biggest gaps first — those are your highest-priority action items.

## Mentioned vs Recommended

The results table distinguishes between mentions and recommendations:

- **Mentioned** means the AI included your brand name somewhere in the response. It might be a list of options, a passing reference, or a comparison.
- **Recommended** means the AI specifically endorsed or suggested your brand. Phrases like "I'd recommend," "a great option is," or "you should check out" indicate a recommendation.

Recommendations are worth more than mentions because they drive action. A user who sees "check out Brand X" is far more likely to visit your site than one who sees "companies in this space include A, B, C, and X."

Track your **recommendation rate** (recommendations / total mentions) as a quality metric alongside SOM as a quantity metric.

## Understanding Sentiment

Every brand mention is classified as positive, neutral, or negative:

- **Positive:** "Brand X is excellent for this" or "I've had great results with Brand X"
- **Neutral:** "Options include Brand X, Brand Y, and Brand Z"
- **Negative:** "Brand X has had reliability issues" or "I'd avoid Brand X for this"

Negative mentions are rare in AI responses (models tend to be cautious), but they happen — especially if negative reviews or critical press articles are in the training data. If you see negative sentiment, investigate the source and address it through review responses or PR.

## Reading Individual Results

Click any result row to see the full AI response. This shows you:

- The exact prompt that was sent
- The complete AI-generated answer
- Where your brand appears in the response (highlighted)
- Which competitors were mentioned
- Source URLs cited (for Perplexity and Google AI Overviews)

Reading full responses is time-consuming but valuable — it shows you exactly how AI models position your brand relative to competitors. Do this for a sample of 5-10 results per monitoring run.

<Callout type="info">
Pay special attention to the source URLs in Perplexity responses. These show you exactly which web pages the AI is pulling from — if a Reddit thread or news article is being cited, that's a thread you want your brand in.
</Callout>

## The Weekly Review Rhythm

Here's how to use the AI Monitor efficiently:

1. **Monday:** Check overall SOM trend (up, down, flat)
2. **Tuesday:** Review keyword performance — identify biggest gaps
3. **Wednesday:** Read 5-10 full responses to understand positioning
4. **Thursday:** Create action items — citation targets, press angles, entity fixes
5. **Friday:** Execute or queue those actions in the relevant modules
