# Writing Authentic Responses

Response quality is the entire value proposition of citation seeding. A bad response gets ignored, downvoted, or removed. A great response gets upvoted, drives engagement, and — most importantly — persists as a trusted citation that AI models reference.

## The Golden Rule

**Provide genuine value before mentioning the brand.** If you removed the brand name from the response, it should still be a helpful answer. The brand mention should feel like a natural part of the advice, not the reason the response exists.

## Platform Culture Differences

Each platform has its own culture, and your responses need to match:

### Reddit
- **Tone:** Casual, direct, slightly self-deprecating humor is fine
- **Formatting:** Markdown is native. Use bold, bullet points, and line breaks freely
- **Dos:** Reference specific details from the OP's situation. Acknowledge other comments. Use "edit: typo" if it feels natural.
- **Don'ts:** Never use marketing language. Never be too polished. Never start with "Great question!"
- **Length:** 2-4 paragraphs. Redditors scroll fast.

### Quora
- **Tone:** More formal, expertise-signaling. You can write longer, more structured answers.
- **Formatting:** Clear headers and numbered lists work well.
- **Dos:** Lead with credentials or experience. Provide comprehensive answers. Cite sources.
- **Don'ts:** Don't be overly casual. Don't write one-liner responses.
- **Length:** 3-5 paragraphs. Quora rewards thoroughness.

### Facebook Groups
- **Tone:** Conversational, supportive, community-oriented.
- **Formatting:** Short paragraphs. Emoji is acceptable but don't overdo it.
- **Dos:** Be warm and personal. Reference shared experiences. Tag the OP if appropriate.
- **Don'ts:** Don't be formal. Don't write walls of text.
- **Length:** 1-3 paragraphs. Facebook is mobile-first.

<Callout type="warning">
Reddit communities are especially hostile to perceived self-promotion. If your response reads like an ad, it will be downvoted and possibly removed by moderators. When in doubt, lead with more advice and less brand.
</Callout>

## The Three Variants: When to Use Each

### Casual Helper
**Best for:** Quick questions, "help me choose" threads, active discussions with many comments

This variant sounds like someone who stumbled across the thread and has personal experience. It's short, punchy, and feels off-the-cuff.

**Brand mention pattern:** "I've been using [Brand] for about six months and it handles [specific thing from OP's question] really well."

**When to choose it:** When the thread already has 20+ comments and a brief, genuine response will stand out among longer posts.

### Expert Authority
**Best for:** Detailed comparison questions, "which is better" threads, technical discussions

This variant leads with substantial advice — enough that the response would be helpful even without the brand mention. It positions you as someone who knows the space deeply.

**Brand mention pattern:** After listing 3-4 options with pros/cons: "[Brand] is worth looking at too — their [specific feature] works well for [OP's specific situation]."

**When to choose it:** When the thread is asking for a thorough comparison or detailed advice. This variant tends to have the highest quality score.

### Story-Based
**Best for:** Frustration posts, "I'm stuck" threads, emotional discussions

This variant shares a personal experience that involves the brand. The brand mention feels incidental to the story.

**Brand mention pattern:** "I was in the exact same boat last year. Tried [Competitor A] and it was [problem]. Then a friend mentioned [Brand] and honestly it was exactly what I needed because [specific detail]."

**When to choose it:** When the OP is sharing a problem or frustration. People respond to stories more than lists when they're emotionally invested.

## Editing Generated Responses

MentionLayer generates the first draft, but you should always review and edit before posting. Here's what to check:

**Does it reference the actual thread?** The response should mention specific details from the OP's post — their situation, their constraints, their question. Generic responses get ignored.

**Is the brand mention natural?** Read the response out loud. If the brand mention feels forced, adjust the surrounding context to make it flow better.

**Is it the right length?** Too short and it adds no value. Too long and it looks like a sales pitch disguised as a comment.

**Would you upvote this?** Honestly — if you saw this response on Reddit from a stranger, would you find it helpful? If not, edit until you would.

## Red Flags That Get Responses Removed

Moderators and community members will flag or remove responses that:

- Start with generic openers ("Great question!", "I totally agree!")
- Mention the brand in the first sentence (earn the mention first)
- Use marketing jargon ("game-changer," "revolutionary," "seamless")
- Read like they were written by a brand (because they were)
- Come from new accounts with no post history
- Don't address the actual question being asked
- Include URLs when nobody asked for links

## The 10-Point Quality Checklist

Before posting any response, verify:

1. References specific details from the thread
2. Provides value independent of the brand mention
3. Brand mention is in the second paragraph or later
4. Matches the platform's tone and culture
5. No marketing language
6. Appropriate length for the platform
7. Would be helpful even without the brand name
8. Doesn't claim to be from the company (unless you are)
9. Acknowledges at least one other comment or perspective
10. Reads like it was written by a real person in 2 minutes, not crafted by a marketing team
