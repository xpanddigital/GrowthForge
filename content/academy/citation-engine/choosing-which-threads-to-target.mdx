# Choosing Which Threads to Target

You'll always have more threads than time. The art of citation seeding is knowing which threads to prioritize, which to skip, and how to work through a batch efficiently.

## Reading the Opportunity Score

The opportunity score is your starting filter. Sort your thread queue by score descending and work top to bottom:

- **70-100:** High priority. These threads have strong Google authority, high relevance, and active engagement. Generate responses immediately.
- **40-69:** Worth reviewing. Check the thread manually — the AI scoring might miss nuance. A thread scored at 55 could be perfect for your niche.
- **Below 40:** Generally skip. These are either too old, too irrelevant, or too low-authority to move the needle. Exceptions exist for very niche industries where any relevant thread is valuable.

<Callout type="tip">
Don't obsess over the exact score. A 72 isn't meaningfully different from a 68. The score is a ranking tool — use it to prioritize your workflow, not as a hard cutoff.
</Callout>

## High-Value Thread Characteristics

Beyond the opportunity score, look for these signals when manually reviewing threads:

### Google Position 1-5
Threads in the top 5 Google results get cited by AI models at dramatically higher rates than those in positions 6-10. If a thread is #1 for your keyword, that's a top-priority target regardless of other factors.

### Recent + High Engagement
A thread from the last 7 days with 30+ comments is ideal. It's active, visible, and your response will be seen by real users who can upvote it. High engagement also signals to AI models that this is a trusted conversation.

### Competitor Mentioned, You're Not
This is the lowest-hanging fruit. If your competitor is mentioned in a thread that ranks well, and you're absent, adding your brand to that conversation directly shifts the competitive balance in AI responses.

### Open-Ended Questions
"What are the best options for X?" and "Can anyone recommend Y?" are better targets than "Is [Specific Brand] good?" — because open-ended questions invite recommendations.

## When to Skip a Thread

Not every thread is worth your time. Skip if:

- **Thread is locked or archived.** You can't post, and AI models may eventually deprioritize it.
- **Thread is more than 1 year old with no recent comments.** Your response will look odd and likely won't be seen.
- **The community doesn't allow recommendations.** Some subreddits have strict self-promotion rules. Read the sidebar.
- **Your brand genuinely doesn't fit.** If the thread is about a problem your product doesn't solve, don't force it. Credibility matters more than one citation.
- **Thread is toxic or controversial.** Don't associate your brand with flame wars or contentious topics.

## Batch Processing: The Efficient Workflow

When you have 20+ threads in your queue, here's the fastest workflow:

### 1. Filter and Sort (2 minutes)
Set filters: opportunity score 60+, status = "queued" or "classified." Sort by score descending. This immediately cuts your list to the highest-value targets.

### 2. Quick Scan (5 minutes)
Scan the titles and platforms. Skip obvious mismatches. Mark threads you want to target — aim for 8-12 per session.

### 3. Generate Responses in Batch (3-5 minutes)
Click "Generate" on each target thread. MentionLayer queues them and generates all three variants. While they generate, you can preview the threads to understand the context.

### 4. Review and Copy (15-20 minutes)
For each thread, open the response panel. Read all three variants. Choose the best fit, edit if needed, copy to clipboard, and open the thread in a new tab to post.

### 5. Post and Mark (10-15 minutes)
Paste the response on the platform. Make any final tweaks to match the thread's specific tone. Return to MentionLayer and mark the thread as "Posted."

A full batch session targeting 10 threads takes about 30-45 minutes. Done weekly, that's 40+ citations per month.

## Prioritization by Keyword Gap

One advanced strategy: cross-reference your thread queue with your AI Monitor keyword performance.

If keyword "best CRM for agencies" has 0% SOM, prioritize threads ranking for that keyword. You're building citations where you have no existing presence — the marginal impact per citation is highest.

If keyword "CRM alternatives" already has 15% SOM, you can deprioritize those threads unless they're exceptionally high-authority.

## Volume Targets

How many citations should you seed per week?

| Client size | Weekly targets | Monthly citations |
|-------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Single brand, niche industry | 3-5 threads | 12-20 |
| Single brand, competitive industry | 8-12 threads | 32-48 |
| Agency managing 5+ clients | 5-8 per client | 20-32 per client |

Consistency beats bursts. AI models reward sustained, recent presence over one-time efforts.
