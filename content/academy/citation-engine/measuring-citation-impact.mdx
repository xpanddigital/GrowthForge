# Measuring Citation Impact

Citation seeding only matters if it actually changes AI visibility. Here's how to track whether your citations are working and build an evidence-based seeding cadence.

## The Citation → AI Visibility Feedback Loop

The connection between citations and AI visibility follows this chain:

1. You post a response mentioning your brand in a high-authority thread
2. The thread (with your citation) continues to rank in Google
3. AI models crawl or reference that thread when answering related queries
4. Your brand appears in the AI response because it's in the cited source
5. AI Monitor detects the mention and your SOM increases

The speed of this loop depends on which AI model you're measuring:
- **Perplexity:** 1-2 weeks (uses live web data)
- **Google AI Overviews:** 2-4 weeks (uses Google's regularly updated index)
- **ChatGPT/Claude:** 1-3 months (requires training data updates)

## Before/After Measurement

The cleanest way to measure citation impact is before/after comparison on specific keywords:

### Before Seeding
Record the SOM for each keyword you plan to target. Note which AI models mention your brand and which don't.

### During Seeding
Track which threads you post in, when you posted, and which keywords they relate to.

### After Seeding (2-4 weeks later)
Re-check SOM for those same keywords. Look for:
- New mentions on Perplexity (your fastest feedback)
- New source URLs in AI responses that match threads you posted in
- SOM increases on targeted keywords vs non-targeted keywords

<Callout type="info">
Compare targeted keywords against untargeted keywords from the same monitoring period. If targeted keywords show SOM improvement and untargeted keywords don't, that's strong evidence your citations caused the change.
</Callout>

## What a Working Campaign Looks Like

Here's a realistic timeline for a new client starting citation seeding:

**Week 1-2:** Seed 10-15 threads. No SOM change expected yet.

**Week 3-4:** First Perplexity mentions start appearing. SOM might increase 2-5 points on targeted keywords for Perplexity specifically. Other models unchanged.

**Week 5-8:** Google AI Overviews start picking up your citations. SOM increases across Perplexity and Google AI. Some keywords see 5-15 point jumps.

**Month 3+:** Cumulative effect. The combination of 40+ citations across your keyword space creates enough consensus that ChatGPT and Claude start mentioning you at their next training updates.

**Month 6+:** Compounding returns. Your citations are established, your brand entity is stronger, and AI models default to including you in relevant responses.

## What Doesn't Work

Not every citation campaign succeeds. Common reasons:

**Wrong threads:** Citing threads that don't rank in Google. If the thread isn't visible to AI models, the citation has no path to influence them.

**Low-quality responses:** Responses that got downvoted or ignored. AI models weight engagement — an upvoted response carries more weight than one with zero votes.

**Too few citations:** 3-4 citations isn't enough to build consensus. You need 15-20+ across your keyword space to create a measurable signal.

**Inconsistent timing:** A burst of 30 citations in week 1 followed by nothing creates a spike that fades. Consistent weekly seeding maintains and builds the signal.

## Building a Seeding Cadence

Based on what works, here's the recommended rhythm:

**Weekly:**
- Seed 5-10 new threads
- Review AI Monitor for any new citations appearing in AI responses
- Note which types of threads and variants are getting the most engagement

**Monthly:**
- Run a full SOM comparison (this month vs last month)
- Identify which keywords showed the most improvement
- Adjust your targeting — double down on what's working, drop what isn't

**Quarterly:**
- Run a full AI Visibility Audit to see composite score progress
- Compare your citation pillar score to baseline
- Set targets for the next quarter based on competitive gaps

## Reporting to Clients

When presenting citation impact to clients, focus on:

1. **SOM before and after** — "Your AI visibility went from 4% to 17% in 90 days"
2. **Specific examples** — "When you ask Perplexity 'best CRM for agencies,' here's the response — and here's where your brand appears"
3. **Competitive shift** — "Your competitor went from appearing in 35% of responses to 28%, while you went from 4% to 17%"
4. **Direct citations** — "12 of the threads we seeded are now being cited by at least one AI model"

Numbers tell the story. Screenshots of AI responses mentioning the client's brand make it real.
