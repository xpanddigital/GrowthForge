# Pricing GEO Services

Pricing GEO is tricky because the market is new. There's no established benchmark like there is for SEO retainers. That's actually an advantage — you get to set the frame.

## Market Context

GEO is a premium service. It's new, specialized, and requires tools and expertise that most agencies don't have. Price accordingly — underpricing signals low confidence in your offering.

Current market rates for agencies offering GEO services (as of early 2026):
- **Small businesses:** $1,500-3,000/month
- **Mid-market:** $3,000-7,500/month
- **Enterprise:** $7,500-15,000+/month

These are for dedicated GEO management. Bundled with existing SEO retainers, GEO typically adds 40-60% to the monthly fee.

## Pricing Models

### Model 1: Monthly Retainer (Most Common)
A fixed monthly fee for ongoing GEO management. Includes:
- Weekly monitoring and analysis
- Ongoing citation seeding (10-20 threads/month)
- Entity maintenance (quarterly audits and fixes)
- Monthly reporting with SOM trends
- Optional: press campaign management

**Pros:** Predictable revenue, client retention, compounding results
**Cons:** Requires justifying ongoing spend

### Model 2: 90-Day Sprint
A project-based engagement with a defined scope:
- Phase 1 (Month 1): Audit, entity fixes, keyword setup
- Phase 2 (Month 2): Citation seeding, first monitoring cycle
- Phase 3 (Month 3): Continued seeding, progress reporting, action plan for ongoing

**Pros:** Lower commitment threshold, clear deliverables
**Cons:** One-time revenue, risk of client not continuing

### Model 3: Credit-Based (MentionLayer Native)
Clients buy credit packs and pay per action:
- Monitoring run: X credits
- Response generation: X credits
- Audit: X credits

**Pros:** Usage-based, scales naturally
**Cons:** Harder to predict revenue, requires credit education

<Callout type="tip">
Start with the 90-day sprint for new prospects. It's a lower commitment that proves value. Then convert to monthly retainers at month 4. This approach has the highest conversion rate because clients have already seen results before committing long-term.
</Callout>

## Stacking GEO with Existing SEO Retainers

If you're already managing SEO for a client, adding GEO is the natural upsell:

**The pitch:** "We've been optimizing your website for Google's ranked results. Now we need to optimize your brand for AI's recommended answers. It's a different surface with different signals — here's your audit showing the gap."

**Pricing:** Add 40-60% to the existing retainer. If they pay $3,000/month for SEO, GEO adds $1,200-1,800/month. The combined package gives them coverage across both traditional search and AI search.

**Positioning:** "Traditional SEO + AI Visibility = Complete Search Coverage." This makes the combined service feel essential, not optional.

## Package Tier Examples

### Bronze — Foundations
$1,500-2,500/month
- AI Visibility monitoring (weekly)
- 5-10 citation seeds per month
- Quarterly entity audit
- Monthly SOM report

### Silver — Growth
$3,000-5,000/month
- Everything in Bronze
- 15-20 citation seeds per month
- Monthly entity audit
- Press release distribution (1/quarter)
- Competitor tracking (3 competitors)
- Bi-weekly strategy calls

### Gold — Dominance
$5,000-10,000/month
- Everything in Silver
- 25-40 citation seeds per month
- Press campaign management (monthly)
- Review generation campaigns
- Full 5-pillar active management
- Weekly strategy calls
- Dedicated account manager

## Value-Based Pricing

The most sophisticated pricing approach is value-based: what is AI visibility worth to this client?

**Calculate:** If the client's category has 50,000 monthly AI-assisted searches, and a 20% SOM means they're recommended in 10,000 of those searches, and 5% of recommendations convert to website visits, and their average customer is worth $500... that's $250,000 in potential value.

A $5,000/month retainer to capture even a fraction of that value is easy to justify.
