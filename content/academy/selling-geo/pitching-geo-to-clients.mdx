# Pitching GEO to Clients

You understand GEO. Now you need to sell it. The good news: the pitch almost sells itself once you show the gap. The challenge is getting the prospect to care about a problem they didn't know they had.

## The Elevator Pitch

One sentence: **"Your brand is invisible to AI search. When people ask ChatGPT or Perplexity for recommendations in your space, your competitors show up and you don't. We fix that."**

That's it. This creates curiosity and urgency without technical jargon. The natural follow-up question is "Really? Let me see." — which is exactly where you want them.

## The Audit-First Sales Motion

The most effective GEO sales approach is audit-first:

1. **Run a free audit** for the prospect before the sales call
2. **Walk through results** showing their specific gaps
3. **Present the action plan** — concrete steps, not vague promises
4. **Close with the metric** — "We'll track your AI Visibility Score monthly. You'll see it climb."

The audit makes the invisible problem visible. A prospect who sees "You scored 18/100 while your competitor scored 62/100" has an emotional reaction that no pitch deck can create.

<Callout type="tip">
Run the audit before the sales call, not during it. Sending the audit results in advance means the prospect arrives already understanding the problem. The call becomes about the solution, not the diagnosis.
</Callout>

## Framing the Competitive Advantage

The strongest motivator isn't "you should do GEO." It's "your competitors are already doing it."

In every audit, highlight the competitor comparison:
- "DistroKid appears in 70% of AI responses for your keywords. You appear in 4%."
- "When someone asks Perplexity for a music licensing service, three competitors are recommended. You're not mentioned."
- "Your competitor has 47 citations in high-authority Reddit threads. You have zero."

This reframes GEO from "nice to have" to "competitive necessity."

## Handling Objections

### "Is this actually real? Do people use AI for buying decisions?"
Yes, and the numbers are growing fast. Share the data: Perplexity has millions of daily users. ChatGPT has hundreds of millions. Google AI Overviews appears on a growing percentage of searches. The shift is happening whether they invest in it or not.

### "How long does it take?"
Honest answer: Perplexity and Google AI Overviews can show results in 2-4 weeks. ChatGPT takes 1-3 months. Full competitive positioning takes 90 days. Set expectations clearly — underpromise, overdeliver.

### "What's the ROI?"
The ROI framework: "If 10% of your category's buying decisions are influenced by AI recommendations today, and that number doubles every year, the cost of being invisible compounds. The brands that build AI visibility now will be nearly impossible to displace in 18 months."

Also: "We track Share of Model monthly. You'll see exactly how your AI visibility changes, correlated with specific actions we take."

### "We already do SEO."
"Great — GEO amplifies SEO and vice versa. SEO gets your website ranked. GEO gets your brand recommended by AI. Different surfaces, complementary signals, same goal: getting found by buyers."

### "Can't we just do this ourselves?"
"You could, the same way you could do SEO in-house. But GEO requires specialized tools (like MentionLayer), specific techniques, and consistent execution. Most agencies that try to do it in-house end up spending more time than it's worth."

## The Monthly Retainer vs Project Model

Two pricing approaches that work:

### Monthly Retainer (Recommended)
Ongoing GEO management — monitoring, citation seeding, entity maintenance, press campaigns. This is the most common model because GEO requires consistent effort.

### Project-Based
A 90-day sprint: audit → fix foundations → seed citations → measure results. Good for prospects who aren't ready to commit to ongoing retainers but want to see what GEO can do.

Most agencies start prospects on a 90-day project and convert them to monthly retainers when they see results.
