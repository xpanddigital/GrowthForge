# Monthly Client Reporting

Reporting is how you keep clients. If they don't understand the value you're delivering, they'll cancel. A great GEO report makes the results undeniable and the next steps clear.

## The AI Visibility Score as the Headline

Your report should lead with one number: the AI Visibility Score. This is the GEO equivalent of "organic traffic" in SEO reporting — the single metric that tells the client whether things are going well.

**Format:** "Your AI Visibility Score is 47/100, up from 31 last month (+52%)"

Big number. Clear direction. Percentage change. That's your opening.

## What to Include in a Monthly GEO Report

### Section 1: Score Overview (1 page)
- Current AI Visibility Score with month-over-month change
- Pillar breakdown (5 scores with trend arrows)
- One-sentence summary: "Citation seeding and entity fixes drove a 16-point improvement this month."

### Section 2: Share of Model Detail (1 page)
- Overall SOM percentage with trend
- Model-by-model breakdown (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, etc.)
- Top 5 keywords where SOM improved
- Top 5 keywords where SOM declined or stayed flat

### Section 3: Actions Taken (1 page)
- Citations seeded this month (count, platforms, top threads)
- Entity fixes completed
- Press coverage secured
- Reviews generated
- Connect each action to its expected impact

### Section 4: Competitive Landscape (1 page)
- Your SOM vs focus competitors (trend chart)
- Notable competitor movements
- Opportunities identified from competitive shifts

### Section 5: Next Month's Plan (1 page)
- Prioritized actions for the coming month
- Specific targets (e.g., "Seed 15 threads targeting the 'CRM for agencies' keyword cluster")
- Any client actions needed (approvals, information, access)

<Callout type="tip">
Keep the report to 5 pages maximum. Clients don't read 20-page reports. Dense, data-rich single pages are better than bloated sections with filler text. Every chart and number should answer one question: "Is our AI visibility improving?"
</Callout>

## Before/After Comparisons

The most powerful element of any GEO report is the before/after:

**Audit baseline → Current state:**
"When we started 3 months ago, your AI Visibility Score was 18. Today it's 54. Here's what changed and why."

**Specific examples:**
"Three months ago, asking Perplexity 'best CRM for agencies' returned 5 competitors and didn't mention you. Today, you're the third recommendation."

Include screenshots of actual AI responses mentioning the client. Nothing is more convincing than seeing their brand name in a ChatGPT answer.

## Monthly vs Quarterly Reporting

- **Monthly reports** track progress and justify ongoing spend. Keep them tight and action-oriented.
- **Quarterly reports** are strategic reviews. Include the monthly data plus: competitive analysis, industry trends, strategy adjustments, and ROI calculations.

For most clients, monthly reports are essential. Skip them, and the client starts wondering what they're paying for.

## Reporting Cadence

Best practice:
1. **Month-end:** MentionLayer generates the data
2. **Day 1-3 of new month:** Build the report
3. **Day 3-5:** Send to client
4. **Day 5-10:** Discuss on monthly strategy call

Don't let reports pile up. A late report signals disorganization and erodes trust.

## Making Reports Shareable

Your report should be formatted so the client can forward it to their CEO or board. That means:
- Professional layout with your agency branding
- No jargon without context
- Clear executive summary at the top
- Specific numbers, not vague improvements
- Visual charts over walls of text

The client's boss doesn't care about your process. They care about: "Is this working? What's the number? How does it compare?"
