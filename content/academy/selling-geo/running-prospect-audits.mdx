# Running Prospect Audits

The prospect audit is your most powerful conversion tool. It shows potential clients exactly where they stand in AI visibility, how they compare to competitors, and what specific actions would fix the gaps. Done right, it sells the service for you.

## Setting Up a Prospect Audit

You don't need the prospect to be a MentionLayer client to run an audit. Here's how:

### Step 1: Gather Basic Information
Before the audit, you need:
- **Brand name** and website URL
- **5-10 core keywords** (what do their customers search for?)
- **2-3 competitor names** (who do they compete with most?)

You can often get this from a brief intro call, their website, or their existing SEO data if they share it.

### Step 2: Create a Temporary Client Profile
In MentionLayer, create the prospect as a client with a minimal brand brief. You don't need the full brief for the audit — the website URL and keywords are enough for the scanning engines to work with.

### Step 3: Run the Audit
Trigger a full audit. It runs all five pillars and completes in 3-5 minutes. The results are immediately available in the dashboard.

### Step 4: Export the Report
Generate a PDF report to share with the prospect. The report includes the composite score, pillar breakdown, executive summary, and action plan.

<Callout type="tip">
Run the audit 24-48 hours before your sales call. This gives you time to review the results, prepare talking points, and identify the most impactful findings to lead with.
</Callout>

## Walking Through Results with a Prospect

Structure your walkthrough for maximum impact:

### Open with the Score
"We ran an AI Visibility Audit on your brand. You scored 22 out of 100." Pause. Let it sink in.

### Show the Competitor Comparison
"Your top competitor scored 58. When someone asks ChatGPT for a recommendation in your space, they get mentioned. You don't." This creates urgency.

### Walk Through Each Pillar
Spend 2-3 minutes on each pillar, focusing on the ones with the biggest gaps:
- "Your citation score is 12 — there are 142 threads ranking in Google for your keywords, and your competitors are mentioned in 47 of them. You're in 3."
- "Your AI presence score is 8 — we tested 40 prompts across four AI models, and you were mentioned in just 2 responses."

### Present the Action Plan
"Here's exactly what we'd do in the first 90 days, ranked by impact." Walk through the top 5 actions. Be specific — "Seed 20 high-authority Reddit threads" is better than "Improve citation presence."

### Close with the Metric
"We measure Share of Model monthly. You'll see your score move from 22 to 50+ within 90 days, with specific data showing which actions drove each improvement."

## The "Before" Snapshot Strategy

The audit serves double duty: it's a sales tool and a baseline. When the prospect becomes a client, you already have the "before" numbers. In 90 days, the comparison tells a powerful ROI story.

Save every prospect audit, even for prospects who don't convert immediately. If they come back in 6 months, you can show them: "Here's where you were, and here's what's changed (or hasn't) since then."

## Converting Audit Recipients

Not every prospect converts immediately. Follow up based on their response:

### Hot (Ready to Start)
They're shocked by the gap and want to act. Propose a 90-day engagement starting with the audit's top-priority actions.

### Warm (Interested but Cautious)
They see the gap but need internal buy-in. Send them the PDF report to share internally, and schedule a follow-up in 2 weeks.

### Cold (Not Yet Ready)
They don't see the urgency. That's fine — schedule a check-in in 3-6 months and mention that the gap will likely widen as competitors invest in GEO.

## Volume Strategy

For agencies building a GEO practice:
- Run 5-10 prospect audits per week
- Convert 20-30% to paid engagements
- Each audit takes 15 minutes to set up and 10 minutes to review
- The PDF report does the heavy lifting between your call and their decision
